# Jurisdiction-Specific Requirements

Reference this file when the user specifies a non-EU jurisdiction, or when the app serves users in multiple regions.

---

## 🇺🇸 California — CCPA / CPRA

**Applies when:** App collects personal data from California residents AND meets thresholds (revenue >$25M, or processes data of >100k consumers/households, or derives >50% revenue from selling data).

**Key requirements on top of general best practices:**
- Disclose categories of personal information collected in last 12 months
- Disclose categories of third parties data is shared with
- Right to Know (categories + specific pieces)
- Right to Delete
- Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing ("Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link)
- Right to Correct
- Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information
- No discrimination for exercising rights
- CPRA adds: Right to Correct, expanded "sensitive PI" definition, data minimization principle

**Privacy Policy must include:**
- "California Privacy Rights" section or separate California Privacy Notice
- Contact info for rights requests (toll-free number if applicable)
- Date of last update

## 🇧🇷 Brazil — LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados)

**Applies when:** Processing personal data of individuals in Brazil, regardless of where the controller is based.

**Key requirements:**
- 10 legal bases for processing (similar to GDPR Art. 6 but includes credit protection)
- Data subject rights: access, correction, anonymization/deletion, portability, info on sharing, opt-out of consent
- DPO (Encarregado) appointment if processing at scale
- Breach notification within "reasonable time" (guidance suggests 2 working days to ANPD)
- International transfers: only to adequate countries or via contractual safeguards

**Differences from GDPR:**
- No extraterritorial scope based on "establishment" — based on where data subjects are
- "Legitimate interest" has narrower application
- No concept of "supervisory authority complaint" right in the same form

## 🇨🇳 China — PIPL (Personal Information Protection Law)

**Applies when:** Processing personal information of individuals within China.

**Key requirements:**
- Separate consent for sensitive personal information
- Cross-border transfer requires: passing security assessment (CAC), certification, or SCCs
- Local representative or entity required for overseas processors
- Data localization requirements for certain sectors (critical infrastructure)
- Purpose limitation strictly enforced

---

## 🇬🇧 UK — UK GDPR + DPA 2018

**Post-Brexit:** UK GDPR is largely equivalent to EU GDPR but:
- ICO (Information Commissioner's Office) is the supervisory authority
- Adequacy decision from EU covers UK → EU transfers
- UK → EU transfers: currently covered but monitor for changes
- UK has its own SCCs (IDTA — International Data Transfer Agreement)

---

## 🇦🇺 Australia — Privacy Act 1988 + APPs

**Applies when:** Annual turnover >AU$3M, or handling health records, or certain other conditions.

**Key requirements (Australian Privacy Principles):**
- APP 1: Open and transparent management — must have a Privacy Policy
- APP 5: Notification of collection
- APP 6: Use or disclosure of personal information
- APP 8: Cross-border disclosure
- APP 11: Security of personal information
- APP 12: Access to personal information
- APP 13: Correction of personal information

No strict consent requirement in all cases — "reasonable expectations" standard used.

## 🌍 General Best Practices (Jurisdiction-Neutral)

When no specific jurisdiction is identified, audit against these universally accepted principles:

1. **Purpose limitation** — data used only for stated purposes
2. **Data minimization** — only collect what's necessary
3. **Storage limitation** — don't keep data longer than needed
4. **Transparency** — plain language, accessible policy
5. **Security** — appropriate technical and organizational measures
6. **Accountability** — someone responsible, documented processes
7. **User control** — meaningful opt-out / deletion mechanisms
